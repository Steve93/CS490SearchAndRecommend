Andrew Garfield won't be returning as Spider-Man

  • By Leanne Bayley
  • Tuesday, 10 February 2015
  • Tweet

Andrew Garfield is trending on Twitter right now because the 31-year-old actor will not be playing the role of Spider-Man, according to reports.

No more spandex for you, Mr Garfield.

It's believed Andrew Garfield has hung up Peter Parker's superhero suit, and if gossip is to be believed, it's because he has been dropped by producers.

The announcement was made by Linda Ge, a reporter for the well-established movie magazine The Wrap, when she took to Twitter to post: "CONFIRMED: Andrew Garfield is NO LONGER Spider-Man. Someone new will be cast. First appearance in MCU, then solo film."

Sony, who currently own the rights to the Spider-Man films, will introduce the superhero in a new Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU) film, which effectively means that fans will be able to see Spider-Man appear in other comic movies such as Captain America, Iron Man, Thor and X-Men.

Bob Iger, Chairman and CEO, The Walt Disney Company said: "Spider-Man is one of Marvel's great characters, beloved around the world.

"We're thrilled to work with Sony Pictures to bring the iconic web-slinger into the Marvel Cinematic Universe, which opens up fantastic new opportunities for storytelling and franchise building."

Sony will then release a new stand-alone Spider-Man film, to be released 28 July 2017 - sans Andrew Garfield.

The deal comes after Sony's Amazing Spider-Man 2 (featuring Andrew Garfield) under-performed at the box office last year.

Despite a respectable $709m (£465m) box office haul, it was the lowest-grossing of Sony's five Spider-Man films so far.

Andrew Garfield has not yet responded to the news.
